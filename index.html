PUFFINS

A few facts about Puffins

All puffin species have predominantly black or black and white plumage, a stocky build, and large beaks.
They shed the colourful outer parts of their bills after the breeding season, leaving a smaller and duller beak.

Puffin Wings

Their short wings are adapted for swimming with a flying technique under water. In the air, they beat their
wings rapidly (up to 400 times per minute) in swift flight, often flying low over the ocean's surface.
